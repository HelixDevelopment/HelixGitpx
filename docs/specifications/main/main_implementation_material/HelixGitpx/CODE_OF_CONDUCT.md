# Code of Conduct

HelixGitpx is committed to being a welcoming, respectful, and productive community for contributors and users regardless of experience level, background, identity, or affiliation. This Code of Conduct governs behaviour in all HelixGitpx-operated spaces (repos, forums, chat, conferences, live events, email).

We adopt and adapt the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) with additions specific to our community.

---

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that foster an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

**Encouraged**:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness.
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Owning mistakes, apologising when warranted, and learning.
- Focusing on what is best for the community — not just for the individual.

**Unacceptable**:

- Sexualised language or imagery; sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting, derogatory comments, or personal attacks.
- Harassment (public or private).
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission (doxxing).
- Discrimination on any protected basis.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or events.
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following.
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviour.

---

## Scope

This Code applies to all community spaces — GitHub repos, issue trackers, pull requests, code review, commit messages, documentation, mailing lists, forums, chat platforms, video calls, and in-person events.

It applies when an individual is officially representing the community, including using an official email address, posting via an official account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it:

- **Email**: `conduct@helixgitpx.example.com`
- **Slack**: direct message any maintainer marked `[Conduct]` in their profile.
- **Anonymous form**: `https://helixgitpx.example.com/conduct-report` (no login required).

Reports should include:
- What happened.
- Where and when.
- Who was involved.
- Any evidence (links, screenshots, logs).
- Any context we should know.

Reporters will receive an acknowledgment within **24 hours** (business days). Investigations kept confidential to the extent possible.

**Retaliation** against anyone in good faith reporting is itself a violation and will be treated as such.

## Enforcement

Maintainers follow this process when addressing violations:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behaviour deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from a maintainer, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code, for a specified period. Includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation, including sustained inappropriate behaviour, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

---

## Appeals

Anyone subject to enforcement action may appeal to the HelixGitpx conduct committee (separate from project maintainers) within 14 days at `conduct-appeals@helixgitpx.example.com`. Appeals are reviewed by committee members not involved in the original decision.

---

## Conduct Committee

- Composed of 3+ members, including at least one non-maintainer.
- Term: 1 year, staggered.
- Disclosures of conflicts of interest; recusals where appropriate.
- Minutes (redacted) of committee decisions published quarterly to build trust.

---

## Transparency

We publish **redacted quarterly statistics**: reports received, actions taken, trends. No personally identifying information.
